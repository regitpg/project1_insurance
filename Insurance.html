4. Answer the following questions with statistical evidence¶

a. Do charges of people who smoke differ significantly from the people who don't?¶

Lets first formulate the Hypothesis.¶

Ho = Charges is same for smokers and non-smokers.¶

Ha = Charges is not same for smokers and non-smokers.¶

Lets now bifurcate the data into 2 groups.¶

In [21]:
smokers_charges = insu[insu['smoker'] == 'yes'].charges
non_smokers_charges = insu[insu['smoker'] == 'no'].charges

Lets now do a Two Sample T-Test for this data and assume a p-value of 0.05. We will reject the null hypothesis if the calculated p-value is less than 0.05.¶

In [22]:
t_stat, p_value = ttest_ind(smokers_charges, non_smokers_charges)
print(t_stat, p_value)

46.664921172723716 8.271435842177219e-283

Since p-value calculated is less than 0.05, we will reject the null hypothesis. In other words - there is definitely a difference of charges for smokers compared to non-smokers¶

c. Is the proportion of smokers significantly different in different genders?¶

Lets first formulate the Hypothesis.¶

Ho = The propotion of smokers are same for both genders.¶

Ha = The propotion of smokers are not same for both genders.¶

Lets now find the propotions.¶

Lets now do a z_Test of propotion to see if this is staistically significant.¶

We are assuming a confidence level of 95% in this case. So if the p-value calculated is less then 0.05, we will have to reject the null hypothesis. Else, we will fail to reject it.¶

In other words - there is definitely a difference of propotion of smokers between genders.¶

d. Is the distribution of bmi across women with no children, one child and two children, the same?¶

Lets first formulate the Hypothesis.¶

Ho = The distribution of bmi is same for woman with no children, one child and two child. In other words, the population mean of bmi is same for all 3 groups.¶

Ha = The distribution of bmi is not same for woman with no children, one child and two child. In other words, the population mean of bmi is differnt for at least one pair of groups.¶

We will be using Anova Test as this is test of means of 3 samples¶

First, lets first create a dataframe of the required sample.¶

In other words, dataframe should have columns bmi, sex(only female) and children (0,1 or 2)¶

In [28]:
